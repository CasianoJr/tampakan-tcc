# Tampakan Community College Series

## Post 1: Introduction to Tampakan Community College – A New Chapter in Local Education

Exciting developments are underway in Tampakan, South Cotabato! The Municipal Government, under the leadership of Mayor Leonard T. Escobillo, RN, is establishing the **Tampakan Community College**. This initiative aims to bring accessible, high-quality, and **free higher education** directly to residents of this 2nd-class municipality in the SOCCSKSARGEN region.

**Key Details:**
- **Location**: Tampakan, South Cotabato (population ~42,304; land area 390 km²). The college will utilize the site of the former Koronadal Academy on LGU-owned land.
- **Vision**: Provide tertiary education focused on skills development, academic growth, and local opportunities. Initial courses may emphasize **Agri-Business**, aligning with the area's agricultural economy.
- **Timeline**: Consultative meetings and documentation progressing (as of March 2026). Anticipated opening in about two years (~2027–2028). Construction actively in progress.
- **Governance**: Fully owned and operated by the Local Government Unit (LGU) of Tampakan.

This project reflects the municipality's commitment to investing in human capital. Mayor Escobillo has prioritized it alongside hospital improvements, water systems, and market modernization.

For Tampakeños, this means reduced barriers to education, lower costs for families, and stronger pathways to employment. Stay tuned for updates!

## Post 2: Mayor Leonard Escobillo's Vision and Leadership

**A Message of Hope and Progress from Mayor Leonard Escobillo**

Mayor Leonard T. Escobillo, RN, has placed the **Tampakan Community College** at the forefront of his administration. Re-elected for his third term, he emphasizes collaboration and prioritizing essential services.

In consultative meetings, the Mayor highlighted completing documentation with input from experts like Dr. Maria Giovanna Abecia. 

**Priorities Include:**
- Free education focused on practical fields like Agri-Business.
- Funding reallocation from festivals to support the college and other projects (e.g., Tampakan General Hospital).
- Community bayanihan in construction.

This people-centered approach empowers youth and builds a brighter future for Tampakan.
